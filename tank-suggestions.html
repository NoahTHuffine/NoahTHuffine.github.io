Cookie-Cutter Tank Suggestions

10 Gallon Beginner Tank

A simple 10-gallon aquarium could include a sponge filter, heater, gravel substrate, and a few live or artificial plants. A common stocking option might be a small group of peaceful nano fish along with a snail or shrimp. This setup is easy to maintain and works well for beginners who are learning aquarium care.

20 Gallon Community Tank

A 20-gallon tank allows for a larger community of fish. It could include a hang-on-back filter, heater, gravel or planted substrate, and several live plants and decorations. Stocking might include a schooling species of fish in the middle of the tank, a few bottom-dwelling fish, and possibly shrimp or snails. This creates a balanced community aquarium with activity throughout different areas of the tank.

40 Gallon Planted Community Tank

A 40-gallon aquarium provides more space for fish and plants. This setup could include a canister filter for stronger filtration, nutrient-rich substrate for plants, driftwood, and a variety of live plants. The larger tank size allows for bigger schools of fish and more stable water conditions. It is ideal for aquarists who want a visually impressive aquarium with a healthy planted ecosystem.
