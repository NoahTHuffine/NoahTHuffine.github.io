Example Aquarium Setup

20 Gallon Community Tank

Tank & Placement

The tank is a standard 20-gallon glass aquarium placed on a sturdy stand in a low-traffic area away from direct sunlight and vents. This helps prevent temperature fluctuations and limits algae growth. The tank is positioned near an outlet for easy connection of equipment.

Substrate

A layer of dark natural gravel about 2 inches deep is used. This provides a good surface for beneficial bacteria to grow and allows waste to settle where it can be removed during maintenance. The darker color also makes fish colors stand out more.

Filtration & Equipment

The tank uses a hang-on-back filter rated for 20–30 gallons, providing both mechanical and biological filtration. Inside the filter, a sponge and bio-media are used instead of disposable cartridges to support beneficial bacteria. A 100-watt adjustable heater keeps the water stable at around 76°F (24°C), and a thermometer is placed on the opposite side of the tank to monitor temperature. An LED light is mounted above the tank and set on a timer for about 8–9 hours per day to support plant growth without encouraging excess algae.

Decorations & Hardscape

The tank includes a piece of driftwood placed slightly off-center to create a natural focal point. Several smooth rocks are arranged around it to form small caves and hiding spots. This layout creates territories and reduces stress for fish by giving them places to retreat.

Plants

The aquarium contains live plants, including easy beginner species such as Java fern, Anubias, and Amazon sword. These plants are placed around the driftwood and in the background to create depth. The plants help absorb nitrates, improve oxygen levels, and provide additional hiding places.

Stocking

The tank is stocked with peaceful, community-friendly species: 8 Neon Tetras (mid-level schooling fish) 6 Corydoras Catfish (bottom-dwelling scavengers) 1 Honey Gourami (centerpiece fish) 2 Nerite Snails (algae control) This combination works well because all species are peaceful, prefer similar water conditions, and occupy different areas of the tank, reducing competition. Water Parameters Temperature: 75–78°F (24–26°C) pH: Around 6.5–7.5 Ammonia: 0 ppm Nitrite: 0 ppm Nitrate: Below 20–30 ppm

These conditions are ideal for the selected species and help keep them healthy.
