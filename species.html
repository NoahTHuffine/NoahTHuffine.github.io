Species Selection & Compatibility

How to Read a Species Profile

Before choosing fish, it is important to read a species profile that provides information about the fish’s needs. A species profile usually includes details about the fish’s adult size, temperament, preferred water temperature, pH range, and diet. Understanding these factors helps aquarists determine whether the fish will fit well in their aquarium. For example, some fish are peaceful and do well in community tanks, while others may be territorial or aggressive. Knowing the expected adult size also prevents choosing fish that will outgrow the aquarium.

Compatibility Charts and Community Planning

Compatibility charts help aquarists determine which fish species can live together peacefully. These charts compare species based on their temperament, water parameter requirements, and behavior. When planning a community tank, it is important to choose fish that prefer similar temperatures, pH levels, and water hardness. Fish that occupy different areas of the tank, such as top, middle, and bottom swimmers, can also help create a balanced and active aquarium without excessive competition for space.

Choosing Fish and Other Inhabitants

In addition to fish, many aquariums include other aquatic animals such as shrimp, snails, or other invertebrates. These animals can add variety and may also help with cleaning tasks such as eating algae or leftover food. For example, snails and shrimp are commonly used in planted tanks because they help recycle organic waste. When choosing tank inhabitants, it is important to make sure they are compatible with the fish and that none of the species will prey on the others.

Avoid Overcrowding

Overcrowding occurs when too many fish are placed in a tank, leading to poor water quality and increased stress. Each fish produces waste that adds to the ammonia levels in the aquarium, and too many fish can overwhelm the filtration system. Overcrowding can also cause aggression and competition for food and territory. Careful planning and researching the adult size of each species can help ensure that the aquarium remains balanced and healthy.
